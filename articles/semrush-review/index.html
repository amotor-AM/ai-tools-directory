What is Semrush?

Semrush is an all-in-one digital marketing platform founded in 2008, headquartered in Boston. Originally focused on SEO, it has expanded to cover:

  • SEO Tools: Keyword research, rank tracking, site audits, backlink analysis
  • Content Marketing: Content optimization, topic research, SEO Writing Assistant
  • Competitive Research: Competitor analysis, traffic analytics, ad intelligence
  • PPC & Advertising: Ad research, keyword suggestions, campaign management
  • Social Media: Social media management, scheduling, analytics
  • Market Research: Market trends, audience insights, traffic analytics

For most users, Semrush is primarily an SEO powerhouse with additional marketing features.

Core Features Breakdown

1. Keyword Research (5/5)

Semrush's keyword research is industry-leading with 25+ billion keywords across 130+ countries.

Key Tools:

  • Keyword Overview: Search volume, CPC, difficulty, SERP features, trends
  • Keyword Magic Tool: Generate thousands of keyword ideas from a seed keyword
  • Keyword Gap: Find keywords competitors rank for that you don't
  • Keyword Manager: Organize keywords into lists for campaigns
  • Organic Traffic Insights: Combine GSC data with Semrush for deeper insights

What Makes It Great:

  • Historical data (10+ years of keyword trends)
  • Local keyword data (city/state level)
  • Question-based keywords
  • Related and long-tail keyword suggestions
  • Integration with other Semrush tools

Limitations:

  • Search volume accuracy varies by country (best for US/UK/Canada)
  • Keyword difficulty score is directional, not absolute

Rating: 5/5 — Best keyword research tool outside of Ahrefs

2. Rank Tracking (5/5)

Track your website's search engine rankings for target keywords across desktop, mobile, and local search.

Key Features:

  • Position Tracking: Monitor daily rankings for unlimited keywords
  • SERP Features: Track featured snippets, People Also Ask, local packs
  • Competitor Tracking: Compare your rankings against competitors
  • Cannibalization Report: Identify pages competing for same keywords
  • Local Tracking: Track rankings by city, ZIP code, or custom location
  • Mobile vs. Desktop: Separate tracking for different devices

What Makes It Great:

  • Daily updates (some competitors update weekly)
  • Historical ranking data
  • Integration with Google Analytics and Search Console
  • Customizable ranking dashboards

Limitations:

  • Keyword tracking limits (500 on Pro, 1,500 on Guru, 5,000 on Business)
  • Costs increase with more keywords

Rating: 5/5 — Excellent rank tracking with daily updates

3. Site Audit (5/5)

Comprehensive technical SEO audit with 140+ checks covering crawlability, performance, security, and on-page SEO.

What It Checks:

  • Crawlability and indexability issues
  • Broken links (internal and external)
  • HTTPS and security issues
  • Page speed and Core Web Vitals
  • Structured data errors
  • Duplicate content
  • Mobile usability
  • International SEO (hreflang)
  • XML sitemap errors

Outputs:

  • Overall site health score (0-100)
  • Prioritized issue list (errors, warnings, notices)
  • Detailed recommendations with fix instructions
  • Historical tracking (see improvements over time)

What Makes It Great:

  • 140+ checks (more comprehensive than most competitors)
  • Easy-to-understand visual reports
  • Thematic reports (crawlability, HTTPS, site performance, etc.)
  • Scheduled audits (weekly/monthly automatic scans)

Limitations:

  • Crawl limits (100K pages on Pro, 300K on Guru, 1M on Business)
  • Some advanced checks require manual validation

Rating: 5/5 — Best site audit tool for identifying technical SEO issues

4. Backlink Analysis (4/5)

Analyze your backlink profile and competitors' backlinks to find link-building opportunities.

Key Tools:

  • Backlink Analytics: Complete backlink profile analysis
  • Backlink Audit: Identify toxic backlinks that could harm rankings
  • Link Building Tool: Find link prospects and manage outreach
  • Backlink Gap: Discover where competitors have links you don't

Backlink Database:

  • 43+ trillion backlinks
  • 808+ million domains
  • Daily updates

What Makes It Great:

  • Toxic backlink detection with disavow file generation
  • Historical backlink data
  • Lost and gained backlink tracking
  • Anchor text analysis

Limitations:

  • Backlink index smaller than Ahrefs (Ahrefs: 35+ trillion)
  • Some backlinks may be outdated or inaccurate
  • Link building tool workflow is clunky

Rating: 4/5 — Excellent backlink analysis, but Ahrefs has a larger index

5. Competitive Analysis (5/5)

Spy on competitors' SEO, PPC, and content strategies.

Key Tools:

  • Domain Overview: Traffic, keywords, backlinks, top pages for any domain
  • Traffic Analytics: Estimate competitors' traffic sources and audience
  • Keyword Gap: Find keywords competitors rank for that you don't
  • Backlink Gap: Discover link-building opportunities
  • Advertising Research: See competitors' Google Ads campaigns and budgets

What Makes It Great:

  • Comprehensive competitive intelligence in one place
  • Reverse-engineer successful competitor strategies
  • Identify quick-win opportunities (gaps you can exploit)

Limitations:

  • Traffic estimates can be inaccurate (especially for smaller sites)
  • Data quality varies by region

Rating: 5/5 — Best competitive analysis tools in the industry

6. Content Marketing Platform (4/5)

Plan, create, and optimize content for SEO.

Key Tools:

  • Topic Research: Discover trending topics and content ideas
  • SEO Writing Assistant: Real-time content optimization (Grammarly-style)
  • SEO Content Template: Generate content briefs from target keywords
  • Post Tracking: Monitor content performance after publishing
  • Content Audit: Analyze existing content performance

What Makes It Great:

  • Writing Assistant integrates with Google Docs and WordPress
  • Real-time SEO, readability, tone of voice, and plagiarism checks
  • Topic Research discovers content gaps
  • All-in-one content workflow

Limitations:

  • Writing Assistant less sophisticated than dedicated tools (Frase, Surfer SEO)
  • No AI content generation (unlike competitors)
  • Content brief automation weaker than Frase

Rating: 4/5 — Good content tools, but specialized tools (Frase, Surfer SEO) are better

7. PPC & Advertising Tools (4/5)

Research and optimize Google Ads and display advertising campaigns.

Key Tools:

  • Advertising Research: Analyze competitors' Google Ads
  • PLA Research: Product Listing Ads analysis for ecommerce
  • Display Advertising: Competitor banner ad analysis
  • Keyword Gap (PPC): Find PPC keyword opportunities

What Makes It Great:

  • See competitors' ad copy, landing pages, and budgets
  • Identify high-converting PPC keywords
  • Reduce wasted ad spend

Limitations:

  • Not a replacement for Google Ads itself
  • Data accuracy varies
  • Most valuable for competitive research, not campaign management

Rating: 4/5 — Excellent PPC research, but not a campaign management platform

8. Local SEO (4/5)

Manage local SEO, Google Business Profile, and local citations.

Key Tools:

  • Listing Management: Manage business info across 150+ directories
  • Position Tracking (Local): Track local pack rankings by location
  • Review Management: Monitor and respond to reviews across platforms

What Makes It Great:

  • Centralize local SEO management
  • Track local pack rankings
  • Automate citation building

Limitations:

  • Listing Management costs extra ($20-40/mo per location)
  • Not as specialized as dedicated local SEO tools (BrightLocal, Whitespark)

Rating: 4/5 — Good local SEO features, but specialists may prefer dedicated tools

Writing Assistant is good but not best-in-class. Frase and Surfer SEO are better for pure content optimization.

While large (43T backlinks), Ahrefs' index is more accurate and comprehensive.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Semrush worth $129.95 per month?

Yes, if:

  • You're an SEO professional or agency
  • You manage 3+ client websites or a large site
  • You need comprehensive keyword research, audits, and rank tracking
  • You want an all-in-one platform to replace multiple tools

No, if:

  • You're a beginner or hobbyist blogger
  • You only need basic keyword research (try Ubersuggest, $29/mo)
  • You primarily need content optimization (try Frase, $15/mo)

Is Semrush better than Ahrefs?

Different strengths:

  • Semrush: Better for all-in-one platform, content tools, competitive research
  • Ahrefs: Better for backlink analysis, cleaner UI, site explorer

Most professionals choose based on primary use case (content = Semrush; links = Ahrefs).

Can beginners use Semrush?

Yes, but with caveats:

  • Steep learning curve (plan for 2-4 weeks to learn the platform)
  • Overwhelming number of features
  • Better to start with simpler tools (Moz, Ubersuggest) and upgrade to Semrush later

Semrush offers free courses via Semrush Academy to help beginners.

Does Semrush have a free version?

Free Plan Available:

  • 10 requests per day
  • Limited keyword data (10 results per search)
  • Basic SERP analysis
  • No rank tracking, site audit, or backlink analysis

Free Trial: 7 days full access (requires credit card)

Which Semrush plan should I get?

  • Pro ($129.95/mo): Freelancers, small businesses, 1-5 sites
  • Guru ($249.95/mo): Growing agencies, 5-15 sites, need historical data
  • Business ($499.95/mo): Large agencies, 15+ sites, need API access

Start with Pro and upgrade if you hit limits.

Can I share my Semrush account?

Yes, with limitations:

  • Pro and Guru: 1 user seat (no sharing)
  • Business: Up to 3 user seats (add more for $45/seat)
  • Sharing login credentials violates Terms of Service

Does Semrush work for local SEO?

Yes, but:

  • Position Tracking supports local ranking tracking
  • Listing Management available (costs extra)
  • Not as specialized as BrightLocal or Whitespark
  • Good for basic local SEO; serious local businesses may need dedicated tools
