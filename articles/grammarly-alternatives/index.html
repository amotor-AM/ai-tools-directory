Best Grammarly Alternatives in 2026: Top 10 Grammar Checkers Compared

Last updated: March 15, 2026

Quick Summary

Grammarly is excellent, but at $12-30/month, many users seek alternatives that are cheaper, more specialized, or offer unique features. This guide covers the 10 best Grammarly alternatives for grammar checking, writing improvement, and style editing.

Why Look for Grammarly Alternatives?

Common reasons:

  • Price: $12-30/mo is too expensive for students/casual writers
  • Specialization: Need paraphrasing (QuillBot) or long-form analysis (ProWritingAid)
  • Platform: Want desktop-only (Hemingway) or Microsoft-focused tool
  • Features: Grammarly lacks AI paraphrasing, plagiarism (on free), advanced style analysis
  • Privacy: Prefer offline tools or open-source alternatives

How to Choose the Right Alternative

Choose ProWritingAid if:

  • You write novels or long-form content
  • You want in-depth style analysis
  • Lifetime deal ($399) appeals to you

Choose QuillBot if:

  • Paraphrasing is your #1 need
  • Budget is tight ($4.17/mo)
  • You need citation generator

Choose Microsoft Editor if:

  • You already use Microsoft 365
  • You want free grammar checking
  • You work mainly in Word/Outlook

Choose Hemingway if:

  • Clarity and readability are priorities
  • You want one-time payment ($19.99)
  • You pair it with another grammar tool

Choose Ginger if:

  • English is your second language
  • You need translation (40+ languages)
  • You want ESL practice lessons

Choose LanguageTool if:

  • You write in multiple languages
  • Privacy matters (open-source)
  • You want affordable multilingual support

Choose Wordtune if:

  • You need creative rewrites
  • Tone switching (formal <-> casual) matters
  • You want AI-powered suggestions

Stick with Grammarly if:

  • Grammar accuracy is #1 priority
  • You need it everywhere (all platforms)
  • Tone detection matters
  • Budget allows $12-30/mo
