## What Makes a Great AI Image Generator?

Before diving into specific tools, let's establish evaluation criteria:

### Image Quality
- **Resolution:** Minimum 1024×1024px for professional use
- **Detail preservation:** Fine textures, realistic lighting, accurate proportions
- **Consistency:** Reliable output quality across generations
- **Style range:** Photorealism, illustration, 3D, abstract art

### Ease of Use
- **Prompt engineering:** How descriptive must prompts be?
- **Learning curve:** Time to first quality output
- **Interface:** Web, API, Discord, desktop app
- **Editing tools:** Inpainting, outpainting, variations

### Commercial Viability
- **Licensing:** Clear commercial use rights
- **Content safety:** Avoids copyrighted/trademarked content
- **Brand compliance:** Safe for client work
- **Watermarks:** Removable or absent

### Cost Efficiency
- **Pricing model:** Credits, subscription, pay-per-use
- **Free tier:** Generous vs. restrictive
- **Enterprise options:** Team features, API access
- **Hidden costs:** GPU requirements, training fees

### Speed and Reliability
- **Generation time:** Seconds vs. minutes
- **Queue times:** Peak hours slowdown
- **Uptime:** Service availability
- **Concurrent generations:** Batch processing

## Advanced Tips for Better AI Images

### Prompt Engineering Essentials

**Good vs. Great Prompts:**

❌ **Weak:** "A cat"
✅ **Better:** "A fluffy orange tabby cat sitting on a windowsill, golden hour lighting, shallow depth of field, photorealistic"

**Universal prompt structure:**
1. **Subject:** What/who (e.g., "a middle-aged woman")
2. **Action/Pose:** Doing what (e.g., "laughing while reading a book")
3. **Setting:** Where (e.g., "in a cozy library with floor-to-ceiling bookshelves")
4. **Lighting:** Natural, studio, dramatic, golden hour, etc.
5. **Style:** Photorealistic, oil painting, digital art, anime, etc.
6. **Technical details:** Camera angle, lens, resolution, quality markers

**Example (Midjourney-optimized):**
```
A weathered lighthouse keeper in his 60s, adjusting the lens mechanism, 
inside a historic lighthouse lamp room at sunset, warm golden light 
streaming through circular windows, cinematic composition, shot on Hasselblad 
X2D, shallow depth of field, dramatic rim lighting, photorealistic --ar 16:9 --v 6
```

### Tool-Specific Tips

**Midjourney:**
- Use `--stylize` values: 50-200 (realistic), 400-700 (artistic), 800+ (abstract)
- Add `--chaos` for variety: 0 (predictable), 50-75 (balanced), 100 (wild)
- Use `--ar` for aspect ratios: `--ar 16:9`, `--ar 9:16`, `--ar 21:9`
- Try `--style raw` for less artistic interpretation
- Use image prompts: paste image URL before text prompt

**FLUX:**
- Keep prompts simple (model is prompt-sensitive)
- Specify "photorealistic" for realism, "digital art" for illustrations
- Use "highly detailed" and "8K resolution" for quality
- Negative prompts work well: "blurry, distorted, low quality"

**Stable Diffusion:**
- Use parentheses for emphasis: `(beautiful face:1.3)` = 130% weight
- Negative prompts are critical: `blurry, distorted, ugly, bad anatomy`
- Specify sampling steps: 20-30 (fast), 40-60 (quality), 80+ (diminishing returns)
- Lower CFG scale (5-8) for creativity, higher (10-15) for prompt adherence

**DALL-E 3 / ChatGPT:**
- Use conversational language (no technical jargon needed)
- Iterate through dialogue: "Make X more Y"
- Let ChatGPT enhance your prompt (don't fight the rewrites)
- Reference previous images: "like the second one, but darker"

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Vague prompts:** "A beautiful landscape" → lacks specificity
2. **Over-prompting:** 500-word prompts confuse models (aim for 20-100 words)
3. **Contradictions:** "Photorealistic anime" → choose one
4. **Ignoring negative prompts:** Explicitly exclude unwanted elements
5. **Wrong aspect ratio:** Square images stretched to widescreen look distorted
6. **Expecting first-try perfection:** Generate 4-10 variations, pick the best

## Legal and Ethical Considerations

### Copyright and Licensing

**Key Questions:**

1. **Can I use AI-generated images commercially?**
   - **Yes** for most paid tools (Midjourney, FLUX, Firefly, Leonardo, DALL-E 3)
   - **Check license** for free tiers (often restricted to personal use)
   - **Read terms** for Stable Diffusion models (varies by model)

2. **Who owns the copyright?**
   - **You own the output** on most platforms (Midjourney, DALL-E, Firefly)
   - **But:** You can't copyright AI-generated content in the US (as of 2026)
   - **Exception:** Substantially modified AI images may qualify

3. **Can I trademark AI-generated logos?**
   - **Maybe:** Depends on originality and modification
   - **Safer:** Use AI for concepts, finalize with human designer

4. **What about training data copyright?**
   - **Adobe Firefly:** Only licensed/public domain (commercially safe)
   - **Others:** Legal gray area (ongoing lawsuits)
   - **Stable Diffusion:** Open source, user assumes risk

### Content Safety and Moderation

**NSFW Content:**
- **Blocked:** Adobe Firefly, Canva, Gemini, DALL-E 3
- **Restricted:** Midjourney (moderate filters)
- **Unrestricted:** Stable Diffusion (self-hosted)
- **Commercial concern:** Most brands avoid tools allowing NSFW

**Deepfakes and Misinformation:**
- Most platforms ban public figure likenesses
- Ethical responsibility to disclose AI generation
- Consider watermarking AI content for transparency

**Bias and Representation:**
- Models reflect training data biases (gender, race, culture)
- Explicitly specify diversity when needed ("diverse group of people")
- Be aware of stereotypes in generated imagery

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is AI image generation legal?

**Short answer:** Yes, using AI image generators is legal. Copyright ownership of outputs is complex and evolving.

**Long answer:** 
- **Using the tools:** Legal worldwide
- **Commercial use:** Depends on platform license (most paid tools allow it)
- **Copyright claims:** US Copyright Office currently denies copyright for AI-only creations
- **Training data:** Ongoing lawsuits (Stability AI, Midjourney, others) challenge use of copyrighted images in training

**Best practice:** Use platforms with commercial licenses, disclose AI generation, modify outputs significantly for copyright claims.

### Can AI image generators create copyrighted characters?

**No.** Most platforms explicitly ban generating:
- Disney characters
- Marvel/DC superheroes
- Pokémon
- Other trademarked IP

**Reality:** Prompts like "Mickey Mouse" often get through filters, but:
- **You cannot use these commercially** (trademark infringement)
- **Your account may be suspended** for repeated violations
- **Legal risk** even for personal use if published

**Legal alternative:** "A cheerful cartoon mouse in red shorts and yellow shoes" creates similar (non-infringing) characters.

### How do I avoid AI-generated images looking "AI-ish"?

**Common "AI look" tells:**
- Overly smooth skin (plastic-looking)
- Perfect symmetry (unnatural)
- Generic stock photo vibe
- Weird hands/fingers
- Unnatural lighting

**Solutions:**
1. **Prompt for imperfection:** "slight asymmetry," "realistic skin texture," "natural lighting"
2. **Use photorealistic models:** FLUX, DALL-E 3, Stable Diffusion realism models
3. **Post-process:** Add grain, adjust colors, subtle blur
4. **Iterate:** Generate 10+ images, pick the most natural
5. **Composite:** Combine AI backgrounds with real subjects

### Can I sell AI-generated images?

**Stock platforms:**
- **Adobe Stock:** Allows AI content (must disclose)
- **Shutterstock:** Allows AI (with contributor agreement)
- **iStock/Getty:** Restricted (case-by-case)
- **Etsy:** Allows AI art (must disclose)

**Print-on-demand:**
- **Redbubble, Society6, Zazzle:** Generally allow AI designs
- **Check licensing:** Ensure commercial rights from AI platform

**NFTs:**
- **OpenSea, Rarible:** Allow AI art
- **Market saturated:** Difficult to stand out

**Best practices:**
- Always disclose AI generation
- Add human creativity (composition, editing, concept)
- Understand platform policies (change frequently)

### How do I improve prompt consistency across images?

**Seed control:**
- Most tools offer "seed" parameter (random number generating the image)
- Same prompt + same seed = identical image
- Change prompt slightly with fixed seed = similar variations

**Reference images:**
- Midjourney, FLUX: Upload reference image before prompt
- Stable Diffusion: ControlNet for pose/composition matching
- DALL-E 3: "Like the previous image, but X"

**Style LoRAs (Stable Diffusion):**
- Train custom LoRA on 20-50 images of desired style
- Apply LoRA to all generations for consistent aesthetic

**Character consistency (specialized):**
- Nano Banana Pro: Built-in character library
- Midjourney: Use `--cref` (character reference) parameter
- Stable Diffusion: Character LoRAs + ControlNet
